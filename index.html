Internet Gap
In Indonesia

BACKGROUND

Internet usage in Indonesia has seen significant growth in recent years. According to data from the Indonesian Internet Service Provider Association (APJII), the number of internet users in Indonesia reached 171.4 million in 2019, representing a penetration rate of around 50 percent. This growth has been driven by a number of factors, including increasing access to affordable mobile devices and the expansion of broadband infrastructure.

PROBLEM

One of the key challenges facing internet usage in Indonesia is the digital divide, which refers to the gap between those who have access to the internet and those who do not. While urban areas tend to have higher rates of internet penetration, rural areas and lower-income communities often have limited or no access to the internet. To address this issue, the Indonesian government has implemented a number of initiatives, including the development of a national broadband plan and the establishment of community internet centers.
